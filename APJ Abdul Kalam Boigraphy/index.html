    BIOGRAPHY OF MISSILE MAN

    Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

    Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering, and he made significant contributions to India's civilian and military space programs. He served as the chief scientific adviser to the Indian government and was also the head of India's Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO). Dr. Kalam was a prolific author and wrote several books, including Wings of Fire, India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium, and Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India. In 2002, Dr. Kalam was elected as the President of India, becoming the first scientist to hold the position. He was widely regarded as the "People's President" due to his humble and approachable personality. Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He was awarded several prestigious honors during his lifetime, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan.

    Education

    Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam completed his early education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu. He went on to study physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and then completed his aeronautical engineering degree from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT), Chennai. After completing his education, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist and later served as the director of two defense-related laboratories. He also served as the chief executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program and played a crucial role in the development of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III). Dr. Kalam was awarded several honorary doctorates from universities around the world in recognition of his contributions to science and technology. He was also a visiting professor at several institutions, including the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad and Banaras Hindu University.

    Achievements

    Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam achieved numerous accomplishments throughout his career as a scientist, engineer, and politician. Here are some of his notable achievements:


    1. Contribution to India's civilian and military space programs: Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully launched the Rohini satellite into space in 1983. He also served as the chief architect of India's ballistic missile defense program.

    2. Development of indigenous missile technology: Kalam was instrumental in the development of several missiles, including the Agni, Prithvi, and Akash missiles, which were designed to meet India's strategic and tactical missile requirements.

    3. Contributions to India's defense capabilities: Dr. Kalam worked on several defense-related projects, including the development of the lightweight combat aircraft (LCA) and the development of a long-range surface-to-air missile (LRSAM) system.

    4. Vision for a developed India: Kalam was a strong advocate for India's development and authored several books, including India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium, which outlined his vision for a developed India by the year 2020.

    5. Presidential tenure: Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely regarded as the "People's President" due to his humble and approachable personality.

    6. Awards and recognitions: Dr. Kalam was the recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan. He was also awarded the Hoover Medal in 2008 for his contribution to science and society.

    TWO LEGENDS IN ONE FRAME

    His Relation with atal vihari vajpayee

    Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam had a close relationship with former Indian Prime Minister Atal Bihari Vajpayee. Vajpayee was the Prime Minister of India when Dr. Kalam was elected as the President of India in 2002.
    During his tenure as the President, Dr. Kalam shared a close rapport with Vajpayee and was known to seek his advice on important matters. Vajpayee also supported Dr. Kalam's initiatives to promote science education and his vision for a developed India.
    After his presidential term ended, Dr. Kalam continued to maintain a close relationship with Vajpayee and described him as a visionary leader who played a key role in shaping India's destiny. When Vajpayee passed away in 2018, Dr. Kalam's family issued a statement expressing their deep condolences and describing Vajpayee as a "great statesman and a wonderful human being."

    His contribution for India

    Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam made significant contributions to India in several fields, including science and technology, education, and national development. Some of his notable contributions to India are:

    ~ Missile technology and defense: Dr. Kalam played a critical role in the development of India's missile technology and defense capabilities. He was instrumental in the development of the Agni, Prithvi, and Akash missiles, which significantly enhanced India's strategic and tactical missile capabilities.

    ~ Space technology: Dr. Kalam was a pioneer in India's space program and made significant contributions to the development of India's civilian and military space capabilities. He served as the chief architect of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and was the head of the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO).

    ~ Education and youth development: Dr. Kalam was a strong advocate for education and youth development. He authored several books aimed at inspiring and motivating young people, and he traveled extensively across India to interact with students and educators. He also launched several initiatives aimed at promoting science and technology education, such as the PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) program.

    ~ National development: Dr. Kalam had a vision for a developed India and worked tirelessly to promote national development. He envisioned India as a knowledge superpower and sought to harness India's scientific and technological capabilities for the country's development. He promoted several initiatives aimed at promoting sustainable development, including the Green India Mission and the Energy Independence Mission.

    Overall, Dr. Kalam's contributions to India have had a profound impact on the country's development and have inspired generations of Indians to strive for excellence and innovation.
